English phrases

Today, let's unwind as we boost our vocabulary and learn English with idiomatic expressions and phrases that often leave non-native speakers confused. Here our top 10 favorite English idiomatic expressions, their meanings and examples of how to use these idiomatic expressions.

Phrase Meaning Example
Eat, sleep and breathe something To be very passionate about something that you think about it all the time. My daughter is into music. She eats, sleeps and breathes r'n'b.
Blood, sweat and tears Something that requires a lot of effort and hard work His career isn't based on luck; he worked his way to the top with his blood, sweat and tears.
Raring to go To be extremely eager to do something My son loves football. He's raring to watch the next game.
Hang on by fingernails To continue to do something in a very difficult situation. The situation remains critical after the earthquake as the survivors are handing on by their fingernails waiting to be rescued.
First out of the gate To be the first one to make a start at something. I was the first one to be out of the gate when I started driving to school.
Have one's heart set on To possess a determination to obtain something. I've had my heart set on getting into Harvard for years.
Punch above one's weight Perform beyond your ability. He was punching above his weight when he volunteered for this project since it was too complicated for him.
Sink your teeth into To do something with a lot of energy and enthusiasm. As Jennifer started practicing yoga, she sank her teeth into her practice.
Cut to the quick To hurt someone deeply, to offend them. Charles was cut to the quick when he was accused of stealing from his family.
Bee in one's bonnet To carry an idea which constantly occupies your thoughts. She has a bee in her bonnet about finding a job in fashion industry.

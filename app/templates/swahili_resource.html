Swahili phrases

In this robust Swahili lesson, you'll become familiar with 18 indispensable Swahili phrases for meeting someone new. It starts with simple greetings like "Hello" and "Good morning," and extends to more involved questions like "Where do you live?" and "How are you?" When communications get a little fumbled, you'll have Swahili phrases like "Sorry, I did not hear you" to keep the conversation flowing. These expressions are more than mere words; they act as social instruments that enable you to connect, interact, and build relationships wherever you go.

English Swahili
Hello Hujambo
Good morning Habari ya asubuhi
Good afternoon Habari ya mchana
Good evening Habari ya jioni
Good night Usiku mwema
What is your name? Jina lako ni nani?
My name is ___ Jina langu ni ___
Sorry, I did not hear you Samahani, sijakusikia
Where do you live? Unaishi wapi?
Where are you from? Unatoka wapi?
How are you? Habari gani?
fine, thank you Nzuri, asante
And you? Na wewe?
Nice to meet you Vizuri kukutana na wewe
Nice to see you Vizuri kukuona
have a nice day Uwe na siku njema
see you later Tutaonana baadaye
see you tomorrow Tutaonana kesho
Goodbye Kwaheri

Recreational Activities

Welcome to Swahili Lesson, which is designed to familiarize you with the Swahili vocabulary surrounding recreational activities. These activities range from adrenaline-pumping adventures like diving and surfing to more leisurely pursuits such as cycling and archery. Whether you enjoy spending your free time indoors or outdoors, this set of words will come in handy.
